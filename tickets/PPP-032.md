# Outcome

A polished, locally playable, sub-three-minute English submission film combines
the verified real-Codex PPP-025 browser capture with a concise explanation of
the product problem, the nREPL-driven authoring model, the full-stack product
evolution, and Codex's role across implementation, verification, deployment,
and production of the film itself.

# Scope

- Preserve the PPP-025 real Codex browser footage as the only generated-product
  demo source.
- Add a reproducible HTML-deck, narration, subtitle, and FFmpeg build pipeline.
- Begin with the installation/Git barrier and explain why PPP is a product REPL,
  not developer hot reload.
- Show Snake, product accounts and visible validation, authenticated SQLite
  ranking, platformization, and Tetris without pretending model waits were
  instantaneous.
- State precisely that the Workspace REPL profile attaches to an already
  running JVM through nREPL while browser behavior evolves in its persistent
  sandbox runtime.
- End with the hosted judge URL and the product tagline.
- Validate duration, streams, subtitles, visual legibility, and secret safety.

# Acceptance Criteria

- [x] Final runtime is below 180 seconds at 1440x900 and 30 fps.
- [x] Video is H.264 with English AAC narration and synchronized English
  subtitles that are both burned in and available as a sidecar.
- [x] Every generated-product frame comes from the verified PPP-025 real Codex
  recording; no fake-provider product output appears.
- [x] Long generation waits are represented by an explicit time-compression
  transition.
- [x] The film visibly covers Snake, accounts and validation, SQLite ranking,
  Game library, and Tetris while preserving prior product behavior.
- [x] The nREPL statement distinguishes live JVM Var redefinition from source
  hot reload and from the persistent browser sandbox runtime.
- [x] Codex/GPT-5.6 contribution includes product decisions, implementation,
  verification, deployment operations, and production of the film.
- [x] No credential, access fragment, product password, session identifier,
  terminal, local path, source tree, or private provider output is visible.
- [x] Final MP4 and SRT remain ignored local artifacts until the owner separately
  authorizes public upload.

# Domain/PBT Tests

- Probe the final media for runtime, dimensions, frame rate, codecs, audio, and
  subtitle streams.
- Validate scene and subtitle timelines are monotonic and end within the media
  runtime.
- Extract a contact sheet and representative full-resolution frames for manual
  visual review.
- Scan narration, deck, subtitle, and rendered frames for forbidden secret and
  local-path patterns.
